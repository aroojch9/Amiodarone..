💊 Amiodarone

📘 Description

Amiodarone is a class III antiarrhythmic indicated for the treatment of recurrent hemodynamically unstable ventricular tachycardia and recurrent ventricular fibrillation.

🧬 Structural Formula

Chemical structure of Amiodarone

🧠 Mechanism of Action

Amiodarone primarily works by blocking voltage-gated potassium channels, leading to prolonged repolarization of the cardiac action potential. It also exhibits properties of Class I (sodium channel blockade), Class II (beta-adrenergic blockade), and Class IV (calcium channel blockade) antiarrhythmic agents, making it effective in managing various arrhythmias.

💊 Therapeutic Category

  • Antiarrhythmic agents
  • Antiarrhythmics, Class II

📋 Indications

  • Ventricular tachycardia (VT)
  • Ventricular fibrillation (VF)
  • Atrial fibrillation (AF)
  • Atrial flutter
  • Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT)
  • Prevention of postoperative atrial fibrillation
  • Refractory arrhythmias

💉 Recommended Dose

Adults:

  • 150 mg IV over 10 min
  • Then 1 mg/min for 6 hours
  • Then 0.5 mg/min maintenance
  • Oral: 100–400 mg/day (maintenance)

Pediatrics:

  • 5 mg/kg IV over 20–60 min (max 15 mg/kg/day)
  • Oral loading: 10–20 mg/kg/day
  • Maintenance: 5–10 mg/kg/day

🧪 Interactions

Drug–Drug:

  • Amiodarone may interact with beta-blockers or certain calcium channel blockers or diltiazem resulting in an excessively slow heart rate or a block in the heart.
  • Also, with warfarin and low dose aspirinAlso, with warfarin and low dose aspirin.

Drug–Food:

  • Avoid grapefruit products grapefruit inhibits CYP3A4 metabolism which may increase the serum concentration of amiodarone
  • Should not consume alcohol and smoke cannabis.

Drug–Disease:

  • QT prolongation
  • Cardiovascular dysfunction
  • Sinus node dysfunction
  • Dialysis

💥 Side Effects

Common: Nausea, fatigue, tremor, bradycardia

Serious: Pulmonary fibrosis, hepatotoxicity, thyroid dysfunction

Visual: Osmosis.org – Amiodarone Side Effects

⛔ Contraindications

  • Bradycardia, SA node block, AV block (no pacemaker)
  • Cardiogenic shock, pregnancy, breastfeeding
  • Hypersensitivity to iodine or amiodarone

🤰 Pregnancy Category

Category D — Evidence of fetal risk (e.g., thyroid dysfunction, neurodevelopmental effects).Only if benefits outweigh risks in life-threatening arrhythmias.Fetal hypothyroidism, growth retardation, and bradycardia

📌 Off-label Use

Off-label indications include atrial fibrillation and supraventricular tachycardia and to prevent ventricular tachyarrhythmias.
